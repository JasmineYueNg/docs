## What is the Web Scraper Tool?

<Frame>
<img src="/images/web-scraper-interface.png" alt="Web Scraper interface showing automated content extraction capabilities" />
</Frame>

The Web Scraper tool enables your AI to extract and understand content from websites. It performs automated crawling and extraction to convert unstructured web pages into clean, structured data that your AI can use for analysis, content generation, or application development.

<Info>
**TL;DR:**
- **Purpose**: Extracts structured data from public websites
- **Function**: Automated crawling and parsing of web pages to provide clean, usable data
- **Benefit**: Powers AI with real-world, up-to-date content for generation or analysis
- **Cost**: Web scraping incurs a Credit Cost due to network and processing resources
</Info>

## Key Benefits

- **Data-Driven AI**: Train your AI on external, real-world data for more relevant and accurate outputs
- **Content Aggregation**: Build applications that aggregate content from multiple sources like news feeds or product listings
- **Structured Output**: Data is delivered in clean, predictable formats (like JSON) for easy AI consumption

## Frequently Asked Questions

<AccordionGroup>
<Accordion title="What types of websites can be scraped?">
The tool works with most public websites. Some sites with heavy anti-scraping measures or authentication requirements may have limitations.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="How is the scraped data formatted?">
Data is typically returned in structured formats like JSON, making it easy to integrate with your application.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Are there any legal considerations?">
Always respect website terms of service and robots.txt files. Only scrape publicly available data and ensure compliance with applicable laws.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="How often can I scrape a website?">
Implement reasonable rate limiting to avoid overloading target servers. The frequency depends on your use case and the target website's policies.
</Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
